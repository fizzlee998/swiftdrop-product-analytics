Problem 1 — Why is revenue declining despite growing users?
Monthly Revenue vs User Growth
Revenue grew strongly until June 2024, then declined sharply — despite the user base continuing to grow. The cause is buried in the discount data.
The Root Cause — Discount Removal
Three phases of discount policy reveal exactly what happened

Q4 2023 20% launch discount applied across all orders. Revenue per user: ₦18k–₦22k/month. Users engaged but subsidised.
Q1 2024 Discounts maintained at 20%. Revenue growing. User growth accelerating. Business looks healthy on the surface.
Apr–Jun 2024 Discounts completely removed (0%). Revenue per user jumped to ₦26k–₦28k — but user engagement dropped sharply and churn accelerated. Many users were loyal to the discount, not the product.
Jul 2024+ 10% partial discount restored. Revenue stabilising at ₦24k–₦26k per user but total revenue still declining as churned users are not being replaced fast enough.

Key findings and recommended actions
Finding 01
Discount removal caused churn spike — not user dissatisfaction
Revenue per active user jumped from ₦22k to ₦28k when discounts were removed in April 2024 — proving core users are willing to pay full price. But marginal users who came for the discount churned immediately. The 10% partial discount restored in July is the right level.
Maintain 10% discount for new users only in their first 3 months. Remove it gradually rather than abruptly. Test a loyalty discount for 12-month customers.
Finding 02
Payment step collapse since February 2024 is costing millions monthly
End-to-end funnel conversion dropped from 8.2% to 2.8% after the February 2024 payment UX change. On 18,705 post-Feb users, that difference alone means approximately 1,000 fewer completed orders per cohort — at an average of ₦4,597 each = ₦4.6M lost per cohort.
Immediately A/B test reverting the payment flow to the pre-February version. If confirmed, ship the revert within 7 days and recoup lost conversion.
Finding 03
TikTok and Facebook Ads bring cheap users who cost more than they earn
TikTok Ads users have an average LTV of ₦22,892 and a 89.3% churn rate. Referral users have an average LTV of ₦47,257 and a 79.2% churn rate. If the cost to acquire a TikTok user is similar to or higher than a referred user, the entire TikTok budget is destroying value.
Pause TikTok Ads immediately. Redirect budget to Referral programme — offer existing users ₦2,000 credit per referred customer who places 3 orders.
Finding 04
19,451 high-risk customers need intervention this month
77.8% of the customer base has a churn risk score above 70. Within this group, E-commerce Sellers and Corporate clients are the highest priority — they have the highest LTV and their loss would have the most material revenue impact.
Launch a tiered win-back campaign. Tier 1 (Corporate): dedicated account manager call. Tier 2 (E-commerce): 15% discount on next 5 orders. Tier 3 (others): push notification with ₦500 delivery credit.
Finding 05
E-commerce Sellers are the highest repeat-order opportunity
E-commerce Sellers average 12.8 orders and ₦60,715 LTV — 17x more than Individual Senders. They are also 22% of the customer base. A dedicated E-commerce growth programme could double revenue without acquiring a single new customer.
Create an E-commerce Seller onboarding programme — dedicated support, bulk pricing, API integration for high-volume sellers, and a loyalty tier at 50+ orders per month.
Finding 06
Same-Day delivery has the highest drop-off but also highest revenue per order
Same-Day delivery generates ₦4,672 average per order — second only to Interstate. But it has the lowest repeat rate (3.4 orders per user) and an 88% delivery success rate — the lowest of all delivery types. Failed Same-Day deliveries destroy customer trust.
Reduce Same-Day delivery zone to areas where 95%+ on-time delivery is achievable. Overcommitting and failing is worse than not offering the service.
